Modular Kitchen Layout Styles

Among the most essential elements of creating your modular kitchen is preparing your countertop layout. In order to develop an efficient kitchen that lets you prepare and walk around easily, you’ll require taking into account both your individual preferences, along with the type of space you have available. Here’s a handy guide explaining 6 of the most common kind of kitchen layouts and the circumstances they are the best fit for.

1.Straight Modular Kitchen Layout

As the name implies, a straight kitchen layout has all the cabinets and shelves placed along a single line. This is suited for a small kitchen space and small apartments that have open floor plan where the living area merges with the kitchen space. Straight layout is the perfect solution for saving space and for ease of cooking. Due to its simple design, it can be altered easily and the linear layout makes it look elegant too.

2.L-Shaped Modular Kitchen Layout

As the name implies, a straight kitchen layout has all the cabinets and shelves placed along a single line. This is suited for a small kitchen space and small apartments that have open floor plan where the living area merges with the kitchen space. Straight layout is the perfect solution for saving space and for ease of cooking. Due to its simple design, it can be altered easily and the linear layout makes it look elegant too.

3.U-Shaped Modular Kitchen Layout

U-shaped types of kitchen layouts are best suited if you have a large dedicated kitchen space. This layout has the kitchen counter along the three walls of the kitchen, making a U shape. You can use these three kitchen counters as per your needs and preferences. For a nuclear family, you can use one counter for cooking top, other for cabinets and the 3rd for sink and other kitchen appliances. For a joint family, you can use 2 counters for cooking and one counter for sink. The possibilities are endless. With a U-shaped kitchen layout, you get a huge amount of storage and counter space with the utmost efficient work triangle.

4.Parallel or Galley Kitchen Layout

Parallel kitchen layout makes optimum use of a long and narrow designated kitchen area. The layout has 2 kitchen counters on the walls opposite to each other. The counters can be equal length or vary in size. The counters have cabinets on tops for storage. This provides an efficient work space, ample storage and separate cooking spaces for different types of cooking. For example, if you are into baking, you can set up your baking equipment on one side and conventional cooktop on other. Or you can use one counter for preparation like slicing and dicing purposes and the other for cooking food.

5.Island Modular Kitchen Layout

Island modular kitchen is a variation and addition to different types of kitchen layouts. If you have plenty of space and already have a straight, L-shaped or U-shaped kitchen layout, then you can install an island counter to create this type of kitchen layout. The island counter can be used for preparation purposes, for baking or to lay out prepared food dishes. You can also use it as a quick breakfast counter or for having your morning coffee in peace.

6.G-Shaped or Peninsula Modular Kitchen

The G-shaped kitchen is a combination of U-shaped kitchen and an island kitchen layout. Instead of a separate installation, here the “island” or the extra working space is connected to the main counter, thus creating the “peninsula”. It uses less floor space than island layout but provides the same benefits of the extra working area. It is accessible from 3 sides and can be used for preparation purposes, for baking or to lay out prepared food dishes or whatever takes your fancy.
